👥 HOW THE TEAM OPERATES

Understanding the loot route rotation & team structure

🎬 Watch How It Works

This video walks through exactly how the loot route system works — from rotation to submission. Watch this first before reading the breakdown below.

🔄 The Rotation System

Every loot route maker has a rotation number — think of it as your place in the queue. If you're rotation #3, you're the 3rd person in line to receive the next map assignment.

When a new loot route map request comes in, the bot automatically assigns it to whoever is currently next in the rotation. No manual selection — it's fully automated. Once the assignment is made, the arrow moves forward to the next person.

How the cycle works:
🔢 Rotation goes 1 → 2 → 3 → ... → N → back to 1
Once the last person in the list receives an assignment, it loops back around to #1 and starts again. Everyone gets an equal share of maps over time.

Your rotation number is not permanent — it shifts as the team changes:

  • ➕ New maker joins → they go to the back of the queue (highest number)
  • ➖ Maker leaves → everyone above them shifts up by 1 with no gaps

📍 Rotation Ranks

Your Rotation # shown on this leaderboard is your live queue position — derived from the order makers were added to the system. The numbers always run 1 through N with no gaps, so the rank updates instantly whenever someone joins or leaves.

Example with 15 active makers:
Ranks run 1–15. If maker #7 leaves, makers #8–15 automatically shift to #7–14. The next incoming map would go to whoever is now positioned at the front of the arrow.

The leaderboard rank (1st, 2nd, 3rd by total points) is separate from your rotation number. You can be rotation #12 in the queue but still be ranked #1 on the leaderboard for total points earned.

🔔 Away Role & Reminders

If you need time off, get the Loot Route Away role. While Away, the rotation arrow skips straight past you — you won't receive any new assignments until the role is removed. The bot never leaves you with a pending map while you're Away.

If you don't respond to an assignment, the bot will keep sending 24-hour reminder pings until you confirm or complete the route. Ignoring assignments for too long can result in the assignment being escalated to a staff member.

Away flow example: Rotation is at position #5. Maker #5 has Away role → bot skips to #6. #6 gets the assignment. When #5's Away role is removed, they re-enter the rotation and will be next after the current cycle passes their number again.

👑 Role Hierarchy

Your Discord role determines a points multiplier applied to every route you complete. All roles use the same rotation system — higher roles just earn more points per submission.

👑 Head Loot Routes
2× points multiplier on every route
🕵️ Loot Route Inspector
1.5× points multiplier on every route
🗺️ Loot Route Maker
Standard points — base of the team

🍀 Lucky Maps

Every assignment has an 11% chance of being a Lucky Map, which awards 2× points automatically. Lucky maps are fully random — no way to influence them. Just complete the route normally and the double bonus is applied for you.

📋 Route Creation Checklist

Step 1: Mark Materials in Game

Go in game and mark all the materials (take note of the spawns you see there too when in game) you see in the area with their designated colour that can be found on the colour code tab.

An important thing to remember is that some objects such as brick that show in fortnite.gg are unfarmable, that why you MUST always go in game. Please mark metal with a rectangle box. Furthermore when marking objects such as wood or brick your circle does not need to be the size of the object you see on fortnite.gg. Ensure it's instead minimal and neat.

An optional thing you can do is mark the mat count you get from each of the objects as seen down below on the image on the right.

Step 2: Mark Important Spawns

Mark every important spawn you see (such as Chests, Slurp Barrels, Launch Pads, etc.) in the area you are assigned with their designated colour that can be found on the website # 📒・colour code tab. Make sure to include extra stuff (such as Ammo Boxes, Vending Machines, Cash Registers, Vaults, etc.)

You need to include all appropriate items that are of value to someone in comp even mushrooms as they provide comp players with extra shield without the cost of their meds.

Step 3: Create Your Route

Go in game and start from where you were told when assigned the Loot Route, and from there, you make your own route based on Time and Efficiency and Meta. (Make sure to not never make unnecessary stop meaning that you should only go from important place to important place such as from an area of slot of metals (car etc) to a chest or chest to chest.) Example is image on the right which is better than the route on the left as it includes rotation + key mats and a safe so this is an example where to not go chest to chest.

Once you finish checking in game, go on to fortnite.gg and mark the exact path you took, ensure you do not have to many arrows from the lines as every time you change direction whilst using the line that creates an arrow and we want least amount of clutter. The route should include every chest that you looted whilst making the route. You want to ensure that your route is realistic and made for comp players in fortnite. You must test your loot route one more time after you have finished making it to check if it works

Step 4: Create Key Legend

When your route is done, make a black box with all the circles / squares you used in your route on the website # 📒・colour code tab explaining what each one of them is.

Remember that you need to follow the exact order given on the website for the key.

Remember to make your key is straight and well structured to make waves routes look professional. Where text, circles, and squares do no overlap. In addition make sure your key is in decent size to your route and is not too small or too big as we take screenshots when adding these routes to the servers and if your key is too small the text will overlap, etc.

A structure you can follow for the key is shown in the image on the right how we want the key straight and in an orderly fashion that is straight and structured your key should look like.

Feel free to include a self promo of your @ in any socials at the bottom of the key.

Step 5: Submit for Review

Once you finish all of the steps mentioned above, post the fortnite.gg link of your Loot Route in # ✅・done-loot-routes and wait for confirmation / response from @Head Loot Routes or @Loot Route Inspector.

If you are told to, you must follow up and fix any mistakes that are pointed out at all times. If you are unable to complete the route at any time please make sure that you dm or tell the @Head Loot Routes or @Loot Route Inspector.

Once you are told "Good job" or something similar, it means your route is done and you can wait for your next route. :WW:

⚠️ Note: Dragging the Key Legend

If you have any troubles with your key, you can drag the black box layer to fix and reposition elements. Here's how to use the draggable key legend:

📋 Key Legend Checklist

  1. Place the black box in the key first — the black background should be at the back layer
  2. Add all text and colors on top — position your legend items (Chest, Wood, Metal, etc.) over the black box
  3. Drag the black box out — press the white dot in the middle and drag to reposition elements freely
  4. Drag the black box back in — move it back behind all your text and color indicators so it's at the back layer
  5. Verify all colors are visible — make sure you can see all the colors, symbols, and text clearly on the map

Any questions about this? DM the Loot Routes team or Loot Route Inspectors for assistance.

🔑 Key Shape Reference Guide

Below are the different key shape formats used for loot routes. Choose the layout that works best for your route:

Vertical Key

As you can see on the key on the right side, this is best used for routes that are a square or huge in size, or when they'll be somewhat scattered. If your route is forming to be in a way that lets you point it out as a square or huge, use this style of key. Make sure your key symbols are in order and not all over the place.

Double Column Key

As you can see on the key on the right side, this is best used for routes that are a medium-sized shape or when they'll be somewhat scattered. If your route is forming to be in a way that lets you point it out as a double-column layout, use this style of key. Make sure your key symbols are in order and not scattered all over the place.

Horizontal Key

As you can see on the key on the top side, this is best used for routes that are a lot of symbols at the bottom and have minimal colors at the top. When you scout a key after it is less like that or horizontally, thus if scouting out your route is forming to have a lot of cluster at the bottom, this appears at the top side this style of key. Make sure your key symbols are in order and not scattered all over the place.
