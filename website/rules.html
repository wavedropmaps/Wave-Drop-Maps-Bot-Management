From the Founder
Mission Statement

Many of you want to understand my personal motive for starting the server. It's quite simple. I saw people with nothing earned, and fellow comp players blowing ludicrous amounts of money on drop maps with little to no gain, being sold the dream by master manipulators like DestinyJesus and other prominent Fortnite figures preaching about how you need a paid drop map.

In knowledge of this, I set out to make high-quality free drop maps that are even better than the paid drop map garbage you hear about. I also aimed to make Fortnite tips and tricks free, instead of sitting behind massive pay walls of $100–$150 for a Fortnite course. Asking many buyers at the time if they ever made their money back. The short answer was no.

This is the reason for Wave, and why I spend all my spare time on this server, for the ultimate goal.

The Origin Story
Year 9: How Wave Was Built

Year 9. Server was sitting at 2,000–5,000 members. I was spending 6 hours every day after school testing and building Drop Maps. Weekends I'd grind 12 hours straight. That was my whole life for months, no joke.

Meanwhile some 30 year old man is stealing every map I made, slapping his name on it, watching his server blow up to 10,000–12,000 members off MY work. I got nothing. He got everything. Didn't lift a finger.

I called him out. His response: "they're free."

Free. Like that makes it ok to take something I poured hundreds of hours into and use it to build his own empire while I'm stuck at a fraction of his size.

Just because the park is free doesn't mean you get to start charging people at the gate. That's literally what he was doing. Free to use isn't free to steal and profit off. Simple as that.

There were nights my mouse was sitting right on the delete server button. Staring at my numbers next to his. Wondering if any of the grinding even mattered when some grown man could just take it all without doing any of the work.

Didn't delete it though.

My thought was simple: I spend 6 hours and make more maps than his fingers can even copy, since the man is lazy. That's exactly what I did.

Kept building. Kept competing. Outworked him.

We hit 100,000 members. Ten times what he ever got from stealing.

Keep in mind, imagine being right at 3–5k members, seeing someone at 10–12k. Keep in mind the Drop Map market at that time: the max server was his server. I was competing against the number one guy, starting with nothing. Because I started with nothing.

Staff Expectations
Communication & Time Zone Policy

We operate across different time zones. Messages may be sent while you are at school, asleep, working, or otherwise unavailable.

Response Expectations

You are not expected to respond or begin a task immediately. Review messages when you are available, acknowledge them, and complete the task as soon as reasonably possible when you are free.

A response is expected within 24 hours of the message being sent. This allows for time zone differences and other commitments.

Example

If a message is sent while you are at school, there is no expectation that you check your phone or start the task immediately. When you are available, acknowledge the message (e.g., "Got it, I'm on it") and complete the task as soon as reasonably possible, provided you have responded within 24 hours.
