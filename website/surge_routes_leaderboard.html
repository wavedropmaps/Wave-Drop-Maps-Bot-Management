👥 HOW THE TEAM OPERATES

Understanding the surge route rotation & team structure

🔄 The Rotation

Surge route makers are in a sequential rotation. When a new map drops in #surge-maps-not-taken, the bot automatically assigns it to whoever is next in line — it cycles through every active maker in order, so everyone gets a fair share. If every maker is busy, the map is held (⏳) and auto-assigned to the next person the moment they free up — so nothing gets double-assigned or lost.

→ View the live Rotation Queue

📋 Your Workflow

  1. You get assigned a map → a card appears in #claim-surge-route and you get a DM.
  2. React to confirm you've seen it and will complete it.
  3. Build the route on fortnite.gg (follow the Route + Colour Code + Key Shape guides).
  4. Post your fortnite.gg link in #done-surge-routes.
  5. A Surge Route Inspector reviews it and gives feedback.

⏱️ Earning Points (speed matters)

Points are based on how fast you complete the route after it's assigned:

  • Within 12 hours → 20 points
  • Within 24 hours → 16 points
  • Within 48 hours → 8 points
  • Within 3 days → 4 points
  • Within 4 days → 0 points
  • Over 4 days → penalty (−8 the first day over, then −2 per extra day)

✨ Multipliers & Lucky Maps

  • 👑 Head Surge Routes → 2× points
  • 🕵️ Surge Route Inspector → 1.5× points
  • 🍀 Lucky Map (~6% chance) → 2× points, stacked on top of your role multiplier

Role multipliers apply to positive points only and don't stack with each other (the higher one wins).

📋 SURGE ROUTE CHECKLIST

Route, key checklist & key shape reference

📁 Building the Route

Follow these steps in order when planning and drawing a surge route:

Step 1 — Scout the POI

Go in-game and scope out the terrain of the POI you land at. Try to find high elevation with good coverage where you can base and get surge on other teams. Think about which teams will land near you — predict where they'll rotate and where the zone pulls, so you can cut them off and farm damage. Make sure to mark the start as well.

Step 2 — Build the paths

Make the paths for where the team will get surge. Apply what you learned in-game and keep it realistic to how the zone pulls — but not too far out; aim for the middle of the endgame.

Step 3 — Mark surge & danger zones

Mark where you want the team looking for surge, and anywhere you want them to avoid rotating — places where they could get pinched or held in zone.

Step 4 — Add key info & zone pulls

Add any other key info — such as what to do if the zone pulls a certain way, or anything that helps the team rotate and explains your thinking. Include where each possible zone could pull: North, East, South, West.

Step 5 — Test the route in-game

Go back in-game and run through your full route. Make sure it fully makes sense, flows well and actually works — then fix any mistakes before you finalise it.

Step 6 — Colour-code the key

Build and colour-code your key — follow the Colour Code Checklist.

📋 Colour Code Checklist

Follow these steps in order when building your surge route key:

Step 1 — Key shape

Follow the Key Shape guide. Make sure your key is big enough for a screenshot and will fit — don't make it tiny.

Step 2 — Create the black box

Create the black box first. If you don't like the black box you can drag it out and lay everything out your own way — but you MUST place the black box first so it sits at the very back. That keeps the colours from leaking/bleeding through, so the key stays clean.

Step 3 — Write down your symbols

Write down all the symbols you use — it should look something like this. Make sure the symbols are lined up straight with even margins, not out of line, and look orderly.

Step 4 — Add the colour codes

Add the colour code to each part using the Colour Code tab.

Step 5 — Submit

When your route is done, post it in #done-surge-routes.

🔑 Key Shape Reference Guide

Below are the different key shape formats used for surge routes. Choose the layout that works best for your route. Make sure your key is big enough for a screenshot and will fit — don't make it tiny.

Vertical Key

Best used for routes that are a square or huge in size, or somewhat scattered. If your route forms a square or huge shape, use this style of key. Make sure your key symbols are in order and not all over the place.

Double Column Key

Best used for medium-sized routes or ones that are somewhat scattered. If your route forms a double-column layout, use this style of key. Make sure your key symbols are in order and not scattered all over the place.

Custom Grid Key

Grid layout option — versatile for any route size or complexity.
